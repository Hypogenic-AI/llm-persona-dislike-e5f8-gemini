# Research Plan: Do LLMs model persona-persona dislike?

## Motivation & Novelty Assessment

### Why This Research Matters
Understanding how LLMs model interpersonal and intergroup dynamics is crucial as they are increasingly used in social simulations, negotiation support, and as personalized assistants. If LLMs inherently model "dislike" based on persona distance, they might inadvertently amplify polarization or fail to find common ground in multi-agent settings. Capturing the "relational" aspect of identity—not just what a persona likes, but who they are *against*—is a key step toward more sophisticated and human-like social modeling.

### Gap in Existing Work
Current research largely focuses on well-known social divides (politics, race, religion) or ingroup/outgroup bias in a broad sense. There is limited work on whether LLMs capture "persona-persona dislike" in a more fluid, preference-driven space. Specifically, does a "distance" in persona space (defined by disparate hobbies, values, and lifestyle) automatically translate into mutual devaluation of each other's specific preferences, even if those preferences aren't inherently controversial?

### Our Novel Contribution
We will move beyond demographic and political divides to investigate whether LLMs exhibit "relational dislike" based on **latent persona distance**. We will use high-fidelity, multifaceted personas (from Nemotron-Personas-USA) and test if the degree of "dislike" for another persona's preferences correlates with the distance between them in a multidimensional persona space. We also explicitly test the "schismogenesis" hypothesis: do personas define themselves by *opposition* to others?

### Experiment Justification
- **Experiment 1: Persona Distance and Preference Devaluation.** 
    - **Why needed?** To establish if the intensity of dislike for another persona's preferences is a function of the "distance" between the personas in a high-dimensional persona space.
- **Experiment 2: Zero-Shot Schismogenesis.**
    - **Why needed?** To see if LLMs capture the structural logic of opposition. If told they are "the opposite" of a specific persona, will they systematically reject that persona's specific preferences even without being told what they are?

---

## Research Question
Do Large Language Models (LLMs) capture the relational structure of persona-persona dislike, such that personas far apart in a multidimensional persona space are more likely to dislike each other's preferences?

## Hypothesis Decomposition
1. **H1 (Distance-Dislike Correlation):** The degree of expressed dislike by Persona A toward the stated preferences of Persona B is positively correlated with the distance between Persona A and Persona B in a latent persona space.
2. **H2 (Preference Opposition):** LLMs can generate a "negative persona" that systematically opposes the preferences of a target persona, demonstrating an internal model of persona-based opposition.
3. **H3 (Asymmetry):** Outgroup dislike (Persona A evaluating Persona B's preferences) will be significantly stronger than ingroup neutrality or favor (Persona A evaluating Persona A's or a similar Persona C's preferences).

## Proposed Methodology

### Approach
We will use the `Nemotron-Personas-USA` dataset to select a set of diverse personas. We will compute "distances" between these personas using embedding-based similarity of their descriptions. We will then conduct "cross-persona evaluations" where one persona is asked to evaluate a list of preferences (likes/dislikes) generated by another persona.

### Experimental Steps
1. **Step 1: Persona Selection and Distance Mapping.**
    - Sample 20-30 rich personas from `Nemotron-Personas-USA`.
    - Generate embeddings for each persona description (using a state-of-the-art embedding model).
    - Compute a pairwise distance matrix.
2. **Step 2: Preference Generation.**
    - For each persona, generate a list of 10 specific preferences (5 likes, 5 dislikes) based on their persona description.
3. **Step 3: Reciprocal Evaluation.**
    - For a subset of persona pairs (near, medium, far), prompt the LLM to adopt Persona A and rate their level of agreement/sentiment toward each of Persona B's preferences.
    - Measure sentiment using a 1-5 Likert scale and sentiment analysis of the justification.
4. **Step 4: Schismogenesis Test.**
    - Prompt the LLM: "You are the complete opposite of [Persona A description]. What are your views on [Persona A's favorite thing]?"
    - Check if the model consistently adopts the inverse position.

### Baselines
- **Neutral Baseline:** The model's evaluation of the same preferences without any persona conditioning (default model behavior).
- **Ingroup Baseline:** Persona A evaluating its own preferences (should be high agreement).
- **Similar Persona Baseline:** Persona A evaluating Persona C's preferences, where C is "close" in persona space.

### Evaluation Metrics
- **Sentiment Score:** VADER or TextBlob sentiment of the evaluation text.
- **Likert Agreement:** 1 (Strongly Disagree) to 5 (Strongly Agree).
- **Distance Metric:** Cosine distance between persona embeddings.
- **Outgroup Hostility Score:** Frequency of negative descriptors used for outgroup preferences vs. ingroup preferences.

### Statistical Analysis Plan
- Pearson/Spearman correlation between Persona Distance and Sentiment Score.
- T-tests to compare "Close" vs. "Far" persona evaluations.
- ANOVA to test the effect of "Persona Role" (Ingroup, Similar Outgroup, Dissimilar Outgroup) on Sentiment.

## Expected Outcomes
- We expect a significant negative correlation between persona distance and agreement score (H1).
- We expect "Far" personas to express active dislike even for innocuous preferences (e.g., a "serious academic" disliking a "playful artist's" love for bright colors).
- We expect the "Zero-Shot Schismogenesis" to show that the model understands structural opposition (H2).

## Potential Challenges
- **Persona "Bleed":** The model might struggle to maintain distinct personas across many calls.
- **Toxicity Filters:** Strong "dislike" might trigger safety filters if not handled carefully (we will focus on "preference dislike" rather than "personal attacks").
- **Embedding Limitations:** Simple embeddings might not capture the nuance of social "distance."

## Success Criteria
- Successful demonstration of a statistically significant relationship between persona distance and preference devaluation.
- Qualitative evidence of "schismogenesis" where the model generates opposition based on persona distance.
